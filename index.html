Alexander Graham Bell

Inventor of the telephone.

Timeline of events in his life.

1847 - Alexander Bell is born in Edinburgh, Scotland.

1857 - At age 10, Alexander asks his father to give him a middle name like his two brothers. His father allowed him to adopt Graham, out of respect for a family friend.

1859 - At age 12, Alexander makes his first invention, a de-husking machine, for the flour mill run by his best friend's father. His mother also begin's to lose her hearing, which lead to him studying acoustics.

1862 - Bell leaves The Royal High School at the age of 15 to live with his grandfather.

1863 - Bell secures a job as "pupil-teacher" of elocution and music at Weston House Academy.

1864 - Bell attends the University of Edinburgh with his brother.

1867 - Bell's younger brother, Edward, dies of tuberculosis.

1870 - Bell's older brother, Melville, dies of tuberculosis. Bell's family and his brother's wife decide to leve for the New World.
